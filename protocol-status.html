Protocol Status

Under the cross-repo integration programme, AETHERIA publishes this interoperable view of the AEP 2.8 governance stack and the dynAEP temporal/runtime layer. Both protocols are used to give citizens deterministic transparency, auditable automation, and sovereign control over agent services, records, and digital interactions.

AEP 2.8 Protocol Stack

Base Node

Mandatory local governance daemon that owns registry, sockets, and policy lifetime. It validates every config change at boot and keeps the governance substrate resident on the local node.

Lattice Channels

Every SDK, connector, wizard, and runtime module communicates via PQEncryptedCapsule sealed frames on lattice transport. Plain wire formats are rejected to prevent side-channel anomalies.

Hyperlattice Wrap

One hyperlattice per governed system. It binds the scene graph, action_path registry, GAP policy nodes, and Lattice Channel transport into one topological matrix.

AgentMesh & Identity

SPIFFE/DID/mTLS identity on lattice transport, governed attestation, and ring-capability checks before any agent action crosses the wrap.

POTOMITAN

Mesh fallback transport. When normal internet is unavailable, the same Lattice Channel frames can route through POTOMITAN without altering governance semantics.

Policy & GAP

Hyperlattice GAP policies, presets, and subprotocol validators govern everything at runtime. Policy loads at boot; nothing runs ungoverned.

CAW Framework

Execution-layer sandbox for shell, file, network, and LLM workloads. Profiles are authored in GAP, compiled locally, and enforced at runtime.

Conformance & SDKs

A public CC-01..CC-15 conformance battery proves compliance, while TypeScript/Python SDKs provide thin, lattice-gated clients.

Evaluation & Trust

15-Step Evaluation Chain

Every agent action executes a deterministic allow/deny chain that covers session state, ring capability, rate limits, intent drift, covenants, Rego checks, content scanners, and lattice validation.

Trust Rings

Four trust tiers gate access progressively: sandbox, user, system, and enterprise. Trust scoring, kill switches, and soft/hard violation handling keep operations recoverable.

Evidence Ledger

SHA-256 hash-chain evidence and Merkle proof bundles make every mutation auditable, which is essential for citizen-facing transparency records.

dynAEP Temporal Layer

Action Lattice

dynAEP extends AEP from static scene governance into runtime event governance. Every event crosses a partial-order DAG that checks lattice membership, parent closure, constraints, trust floor, and agent interest before delivery.

Temporal Authority (dynAEP-TA)

The bridge clock is the authoritative time source. PTP, NTP, and system-clock fallback are used with drift, future, and staleness checks to prevent unreliable timestamps from corrupting causal chains.

Perception Governance (dynAEP-TA-P)

Speech pacing, haptic duration, notification cadence, and sensor polling intervals are governed against research-derived perception bounds before they reach citizens.

Observer Adapters & MCP

Webhook, SSE, poll, blockchain, and email adapters normalize external events into LatticeEvents. A native MCP server exposes lattice queries, validation, agent registration, discovery, event submission, temporal queries, and causal tracing.

What This Means for Citizens & Governance

Deterministic Outcomes

Because policy is enforced as architecture, citizen-facing services cannot deviate from the approved behaviour contract. This reduces surprise, error, and unilateral change.

Auditable Automation

Evidence ledgers, vector clocks, and Merkle proofs make agency automations inspectable. Oversight bodies can verify that each automated decision satisfied the required sequence, constraints, and trust tier before execution.

Transparent Integration

The AEP/dynAEP stack is published as source, Docker, and SDKs with an open conformance suite. Transparency reports and protocol status can be reviewed alongside operational disclosures.

Sovereign Resilience

Lattice Channels, POTOMITAN mesh fallback, local Base Node deployment, and offline conformance testing ensure AETHERIA services remain operable under degraded network states without relaxing governance.
