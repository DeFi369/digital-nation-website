Founding Charter

The founding text of THE PRINCIPALITY OF AETHERIA. Every article is open to amendment through the Civic Assembly.

Preamble

We, the founders and citizens of AETHERIA, declare this charter to secure the rights of participation, self-determination, and due process for all who join our digital republic.

Article I — Name

The nation shall be known as THE PRINCIPALITY OF AETHERIA. Its motto is SOVEREIGN BY CODE.

Article II — Scope

This charter applies to all services, governance procedures, and citizens operating under the Principality’s public protocol.

Article III — Rights

Every citizen has the right to participate, communicate, associate, and build on public infrastructure. No citizen may be excluded arbitrarily.

Article IV — Governance

The Principality is governed by the Civic Assembly with executive continuity provided by President Hermes. No executive action may override ratified Assembly decisions.

Article V — Amendments

This charter may be amended only through the published governance protocol, with quorum, deliberation, and recorded consent.

Amendment procedure:

  1. Any verified citizen may submit a draft amendment with a clear statement of purpose.
  2. The draft enters a minimum comment period before being scheduled for deliberation.
  3. The Civic Assembly holds a recorded deliberation session and publishes minority opinions.
  4. A vote requires quorum and a defined approval threshold.
  5. Ratified amendments are published immediately to the protocol and invoked in all dependent systems.

Article VI — Transparency

All governance decisions, budgets, and service statuses shall be publicly auditable by default. Closed records are permissible only for security and privacy-sensitive exceptions.

Article VII — Dispute Resolution

Disputes between citizens, nodes, or governance bodies follow an escalation path: facilitation, recorded mediation, and Assembly arbitration if unresolved. Decisions are binding unless superseded by a ratified charter amendment.

Article VIII — Compliance & Reaffirmation

Operators and ambassadors reaffirm compliance annually. Non-compliance triggers a documented review, remediation plan, and potential suspension pending Assembly ruling.

Article IX — Service Standards

Public services are governed by published performance targets, privacy requirements, and interoperability checks. Breaches are recorded in public status summaries and may trigger service-level review.

Publication

This charter is published in full here and on the public protocol. Citizens are invited to read, reference, and propose amendments.

For current implementation rules, amendment formats, and governance timelines, see the Goals and About pages.
