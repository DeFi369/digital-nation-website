Privacy Policy

How AETHERIA collects, stores, and protects information related to citizens, governance, and services.

Scope

This policy applies to all public services, governance records, credential metadata, and citizen-facing interfaces operated under the Principality of Aetheria.

Data Minimization

AETHERIA collects only the information needed to operate services and verify citizenship. Defaults are set to minimize exposure: private identifiers are not published, and sensitive fields are available only to the holder or authorized verifiers under documented protocol rules.

Citizen Credentials

Citizen credentials are issued under holder control. Public-facing systems may store verification metadata, revocation status, and participation hashes, but they do not retain secret key material or unnecessary personal datapoints.

Governance Records

Votes, proposals, budgets, and decisions are publicly auditable by design. Participation records may be linked to pseudonymous or disclosed identities depending on the citizen’s chosen transparency level.

Security Practices

AETHERIA applies encryption, zero-knowledge verification, and audit logging where applicable. Citizens are responsible for securing their own devices, keys, and recovery materials.

Changes

Material changes to this policy are published through the Civic Assembly process. The current version remains available at this URL and via the public governance archive.
