Before you request verification, take this self-check. It is not a gate — it is a map. See where you stand today and what to prepare for a successful application.

Passport Framework

Cryptographic ID

You own your citizen ID. It is issued on protocol, migrates across devices, and cannot be revoked without your consent.

Proof of Participation

Activities across governance, services, and contributors build a verifiable participation record rather than a static credential.

Portable Credentials

Interoperable credentials usable across other sovereign networks without a central registrar or credential issuer.

Citizen Rights & Duties

Rights

  • Participate in governance and voting
  • Access core public services
  • Operate a node or contribute to protocols
  • Transparent accountability and audit rights

Duties

  • Follow published protocol and governance decisions
  • Respect the rights and privacy of other citizens
  • Report defects and security issues in good faith
  • Support onboarding and education of new citizens

Paths to Participation

Engineering

Build protocol, services, tools, or integrations. Contribution evidence is recorded and reviewed by the Civic Assembly.

Governance

Participate in deliberation, voting, budgeting, and standards review. All verified citizens are eligible.

Outreach

Support onboarding, documentation, education, and community growth as an ambassador or contributor.

Node Operation

Run infrastructure, relay traffic, and maintain service availability under documented regional responsibilities.

Onboarding Flow

1. Review

Read the charter, manifesto, and recognition policies to understand the obligations of citizenship.

2. Choose a Path

Select one or more contribution areas and prepare a short statement of intended participation.

3. Request Verification

Submit your introduction and contribution plan. The Civic Assembly reviews submissions transparently.

4. Receive Credentials

On approval, you receive your citizen credentials and access to core services.
