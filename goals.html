Goals & Milestones

Measurable progress toward sovereignty by protocol — grounded in real micronation frameworks and Aetheria-specific capabilities.

Montevideo Framework

We align with the UN Montevideo Convention criteria for statehood and adapt them to a digital context: a permanent population, a defined governance protocol, functional institutions, and the capacity to enter into relations with other networks and polities.

Permanent Population

Grow verified citizen registry with transparent onboarding, maintain active participation rates, and publish demographic dashboards by protocol level.

Defined Governance Protocol

Codify constitutional rules, voting weights, amendment procedures, and dispute resolution in reproducible public specifications.

Effective Institutions

Operate durable services: identity, communication, governance, and settlement — each with documented SLAs and public audits.

Capacity to Relate

Establish protocol-level interoperability with other sovereign networks, publish recognition criteria, and maintain observer-state relationships.

Digital Nation Milestones

Operational Sovereignty

Run core services without dependence on centralized providers. Every critical function has redundant, independently operated nodes.

Open Interoperability

Publish and maintain open standards for identity, messaging, settlement, and governance that any sovereign network can adopt.

Universal Access

Reduce the barrier to participation. If you have a network connection, you are eligible for citizenship. No geography, no gate, no permit.
