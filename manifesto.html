Article I — Right to Self-Determination

Every individual has the right to define their own digital existence without corporate or state interference. Access to identity, communication, and association must not require permission from intermediaries.

Article II — Protocol, Not Platform

We choose open protocol over closed platforms. Services should be governed by documented, reproducible standards that no single operator can revoke unilaterally.

Article III — Privacy by Default

Surveillance is opt-in, not opt-out. Privacy is not a feature — it is a baseline requirement for dignified participation in digital life.

Article IV — Merit and Transparency

Leadership is earned through demonstrated contribution, technical verification, and openness. Every vote, budget item, and decision must be publicly auditable.

Article V — Borderless Participation

Geography should not determine access to opportunity, community, or services. If you have a network connection, you can participate.

Article VI — Interoperability

We build for connection, not isolation. Our standards are designed to collaborate with other sovereign networks and to share public goods without new dependencies.

Article VII — Accountability

Authority is a temporary trust. Operators and representatives are subject to regular review, measurable performance targets, and documented escalation paths. Citizens may initiate accountability reviews without fear of reprisal.

Article VIII — Inclusion

Participation must not be blocked by geography, wealth, language, or ability. We design onboarding, accessibility, and translation into core infrastructure rather than treating them as afterthoughts.

Article IX — Stewardship

Public infrastructure is a shared inheritance. Resources are maintained for durability, transparency, and reuse by future participants. Short-term extraction models are incompatible with long-term republic building.

Article X — Conflict of Interest

Decision-makers must disclose interests that could affect impartial judgment. Review bodies monitor conflicts and reassign or recuse cases where bias threatens public trust.

Our Republic

These principles are made real through a layered republic optimized for a digital-native nation. The Constitutional Core holds executive authority and safeguards the charter. The Strategic Cabinet sets national policy across diplomacy, finance, citizenship, technology, infrastructure, and justice. Operational Offices execute cross-cutting functions spanning data, records, risk, treasury, and civic engagement. Service Branches deliver civic and operational capacity through the Digital Service Corps and Electoral Commission. The Assembly of the Digital Nation confirms appointments, approves charter amendments, and holds the executive to account. The Charter Tribunal is the final arbiter of disputes and constitutional questions. Every layer is accountable to these articles and bound by the same transparency requirements.

Our Commitment

This manifesto is not a slogan — it is a binding commitment encoded into governance, infrastructure, and shared expectations. Every citizen, operator, and partner may hold the Principality accountable to these articles.
