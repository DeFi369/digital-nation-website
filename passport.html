Passport / Citizen Credential

The Principality of Aetheria issues a living, portable, machine-verifiable citizen credential — designed for digital identity, diplomatic access, and secure service authorization in a networked world.

Credential Overview

The Aetheria Passport is not a static document or printed booklet. It is a cryptographically signed credential bundle under holder control, portable across devices and interoperable with other sovereign systems. It functions as proof of identity, travel authorization, service credential, and governance token — all derived from a single keypair that the citizen owns.

This credential is used for four core purposes: identity, verification, service access, and passport diplomacy. Together, these layers create a practical trust framework for a digital republic.

Functional Layers

Identity

The credential anchors identity in cryptography, not paper. A holder-controlled keypair issues the public identity, enabling login, governance participation, and recognition validation without a central administrator.

Travel & Passport Diplomacy

In a browser-first polis, travel authorization is expressed through verifiable credentials and mutual-recognition agreements. Aetheria’s passport enables diplomatic transit, event entry, and cross-polity verification through accepted protocol standards.

Access to Services

Citizens use the passport to authenticate against internal services and partner networks — voting, registry queries, treasury interfaces, and public benefit systems — without repeating onboarding or exposing private identifiers unnecessarily.

Verification

Verifiers probe the credential through a signed challenge-response flow. Responses are minimal by default: status, revocation state, and optional disclosed claims. The sovereign credential becomes a compact trust anchor.

Security & Privacy

Key Material Ownership

Private keys remain in citizen custody by default. Credential backups, rotations, and multi-device registration are initiated by the holder and recorded as protocol events — never silently overwritten by an issuer.

Revocation Rules

A credential may be revoked through transparent protocol procedures: legislative decision, holder request, or failed governance obligations. Every revocation is timestamped and verifiable, with the ability to audit intent and evidence.

Zero-Knowledge Claims

Aetheria passports support selective disclosure. A citizen can prove citizenship, age band, eligibility status, or residency level without disclosing the underlying identifier or datapoints — preserving privacy while enabling trust.

Passport Diplomacy

Passport value increases through mutual recognition. Aetheria therefore pursues explicit agreements with allied micronations, digital polities, and open-standard communities to establish bounded but meaningful acceptance of each other’s sovereign credentials.

Mutual Recognition

Recognition is bilateral, documented, and revocable. We issue declarations naming compatible systems and criteria under which Aetheria passports are honored, and we invite reciprocal recognition for our citizens.

Open Protocol Alignment

Rather than locking into a single vendor stack, Aetheria’s credential design aligns with emerging standards for decentralized identifiers and verifiable credentials, enabling newcomers to join without losing continuity.

Transparent Audit

Every recognized relationship is published under verifiable metadata so that citizens, verifiers, and partner polities can confirm status without relying on private trust brokers or unverifiable word-of-mouth agreements.
