Amendment Process

Every citizen may propose an amendment. The timeline below reflects the current standing process, codified in Article V and administered through Assembly procedures:

  1. A verified citizen submits a draft amendment with a clear statement of purpose, the affected articles, and any supporting data or precedent.
  2. The proposal enters a minimum 14-day comment period published to Assembly and visible through Protocol feeds.
  3. A volunteer or appointed review committee classifies impact and schedules at least one recorded deliberation session.
  4. The Justice office reviews the amendment for consistency with existing articles and Court precedent before the vote opens.
  5. Assembly vote opens; quorum threshold and approval margin are displayed explicitly on this page via live data below.
  6. Ratified amendments are appended to this page as revision entries and incorporated into the canonical Charter text.

Cross-Links

Amendments reference the following foundational instruments. Each link opens the governing document or the relevant ministry view:

  • Charter — the current standing constitutional text
  • Missions — operational mandates shaped by or requiring charter change
  • Civic Assembly — deliberation, vote records, and quorum settings
  • Justice and Court — precedent review and dispute escalation context
  • Protocol — implementation mechanics and publication format rules
  • About and Manifesto — philosophical grounding for amendments
  • Goals — the strategic targets that amendments are meant to serve

How to Propose

Ready to contribute? Read the Charter and the amendment rubric on the Assembly page, then submit a draft through the Engage portal. Your amendment will appear here once it clears initial validation.
