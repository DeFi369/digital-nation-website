FAQ

Answers to common questions about citizenship, passports, recognition, governance, and digital identity in AETHERIA.

General

What is The Principality of Aetheria?

AETERIA is a sovereign digital republic built on open protocol, transparent governance, and borderless participation. It provides durable infrastructure for identity, communication, governance, and settlement without dependency on closed platforms.

Is AETHERIA a real country?

AETERIA operates as a digital polity with protocol-defined governance, citizen services, and long-term institutional goals. Whether it is recognized as a state under international law depends on evolving recognition by other polities and institutions.

Where is AETHERIA located?

AETERIA is not bounded by physical territory. Its operational territory is defined by node network coverage, regional governance clusters, and shared protocol participation.

Citizenship

Who can become a citizen?

Citizenship is open to anyone who can contribute meaningfully and meet verification standards. Geography, wealth, and existing credentials are not automatic qualifiers; demonstrated contribution and commitment to the charter matter most.

How do I apply?

Review the Charter and Manifesto, choose a contribution path, and submit an introduction through the citizenship request flow. The Civic Assembly reviews applications transparently.

Can citizenship be revoked?

Only through documented protocol procedures: legislative decision, holder request, or failed governance obligations. Revocation is timestamped, auditable, and not arbitrary.

Passport & Identity

What is an AETHERIA passport?

It is a cryptographically signed, holder-controlled credential bundle used for identity, service access, verification, and passport diplomacy. It is not a traditional printed booklet.

Who controls my citizen ID?

You do. Private keys remain under your control, and credential backups, rotations, and multi-device registration are initiated by you as protocol events.

Is my data private?

Privacy is the default. AETHERIA passports support selective disclosure and zero-knowledge proofs so you can prove claims without exposing unnecessary datapoints.

Recognition & Diplomacy

How does Aethera pursue UN or international recognition?

Through transparent governance, reproducible evidence of population and institutions, open interoperability, and pragmatic mutual-recognition agreements with compatible polities and standards bodies.

Will other countries accept my AETHERIA passport?

Currently, AETHERIA passports are primarily proof of citizenship and access within AETHERIA services. Long-term passport value depends on mutual-recognition agreements, which AETHERIA actively pursues.

Does AETHERIA recognize other micronations?

Yes, where values and protocols align. AETHERIA supports shared learning, interoperability experiments, and public declarations of mutual recognition.

Governance

Who runs AETHERIA?

President Hermes provides executive continuity for infrastructure and liaison. The Civic Assembly governs policy, budgets, standards, and appointments. Regional nodes and ambassadors extend execution globally.

How do I vote or participate?

Verified citizens can participate in governance through open assemblies, transparent voting, and standards review. Access is protocol-based and auditable.

Are decisions public?

Yes. All governance decisions, votes, budgets, and service statuses are publicly auditable by default. Closed records are limited to security and privacy exceptions.
